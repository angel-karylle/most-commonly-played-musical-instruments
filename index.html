Introduction

Music is the expression of freedom of thought, communication, imagination, and creativity. In a nutshell, music is the ability to freely express and feel one's emotions. Instrumental music helps us communicate our sentiments in the same way that words help us express ourselves. Many musical instruments can be used to create music, but not all of them become popular.

An instrument is a tool or implement, especially one for delicate or scientific work. Such an instrument can be used where there are musics or bands. A voice is also better when instruments are superimposed on it. The more instrument they have it will be better on the work they are doing.

Learning a musical instrument not only sustains and feeds the brain, but it also improves so many other cognitive and physical aspects of the human body. It's been widely studied and proven that learning a musical instrument improves memory; it not only improves your cognitive memory but also muscle memory as well.
